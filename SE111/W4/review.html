Review

Downton Abbey: A New Era is one of my favorite movies because it holds a dear ending for one of my favorite characters. In Downton Abbey, Dame Maggie Smith plays the Dowager Countess of Grantham, Violet Crawley. Throughout the show Maggie Smith protrays Violet Crawley as this cunning and posh aristocratic lady, whose presence she always commanded. She was a formidable character that helped shaped Downton Abbey. In this movie, Vioelt Crawley inherits a Villa in the south of France from a man she had one time fling with. This launches the characters on a journey to France to uncover Violets secrative past, while at home, a film crew has entered Downton Abbey. In hopes of Downton Abbey surviving, a movie is being filmed, giving us and the characters a glimspe of old hollywood. So in a sense, this would've been an almost perfect goodbye to Downton Abbey. We see the characters finding thier true meaning in life, filled with love and hope for the future. Violet Crawleys death signifies the end of an era, and the beginning of a new one. A beautiful conclusion with huge expectantions for Downton Abbey: The Grand Finale out this September.
